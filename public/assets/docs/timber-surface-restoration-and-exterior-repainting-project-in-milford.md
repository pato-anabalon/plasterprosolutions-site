# Timber Surface Restoration and Exterior Repainting Project in Milford

Older timber homes can develop a range of surface issues over time, especially when previous coating systems begin to fail.

This Milford project involved extensive exterior remediation work after oil bleed from aging timber surfaces caused widespread bubbling and paint failure across multiple areas of the property.

The homeowners wanted more than a temporary fix. The goal was to properly restore the affected surfaces, stabilise the exterior, and apply a protective repainting system that would improve both appearance and long-term durability.

  

## Scope of Work

-   Surface scraping and remediation
-   Removal of failed and bubbling paint
-   Timber preparation and restoration
-   Surface sanding and repairs
-   Exterior repainting
-   Protective coating system application
-   Detailed finishing work

  

## Understanding Paint Failure on Older Timber Surfaces

One of the challenges with older timber homes is that natural oils within the timber can begin affecting the paint system over time.

As coatings fail, surfaces may start bubbling, lifting, or separating from the substrate underneath. In many cases, repainting over those areas without proper remediation only leads to the same issues returning later.

On this project, damaged coatings needed to be carefully scraped back and stabilised before repainting could begin.

Surface preparation was a major part of achieving a long-lasting result.

  

## Restoring the Exterior Properly

Our team carried out detailed remediation work across the property to remove unstable coatings and prepare the timber correctly for repainting.

The focus throughout the project was not simply visual improvement, but ensuring the new system had the best possible foundation for long-term performance.

Using quality preparation methods and protective coatings, the exterior was fully restored while maintaining a clean and consistent appearance throughout the home.

  

## A Cleaner and More Durable Finish

By the end of the project, the difference across the property was significant.

The repaired and repainted surfaces now provide a far cleaner presentation while also helping protect the home against future deterioration.

Projects like this highlight the importance of identifying the cause of paint failure before beginning any repainting work.
